Unwrap

Chosen shape

These are the series of shapes I ended up choosing, a rectangle, a cylinder, and a cone. I can't explain why these shapes were the ones I chose other than the overall shape felt familiar and pleasing to me. I chose this shape as my final after deliberation betweeb several other shapes.

Other options

Next I focused on unwrapping the objects. In blender I took their UV maps and flatteneded them out and messed around with it until I had a template that would be easier to cut out and assemble. I figured that this part would end up taking the most time, however actually cutting out the shapes took much longer for me.

Rectangle unwrapped

Cone and cylinder unwrapped

After several attempts to cut out these shapes (awful fine motor skills) I finally had cut out all the shapes without butchering the tabs or cutting off too much of the shape as to make it lopsided. The final assembly has a slight lean due to its light weight and the cone on one side, but I imagine that if made from a heavier material the weight would fix this issue.

Possible Uses:

  1. A handheld watering can
  2. If you were to make some slight changes in the inner working of the cylinder, you could make it a cheese grater, with the cilinder working to funnel out the grated cheese.
  3. You could fill the rectangle with a soap like substance, you could blow through the cylinder to create bubbles from the cone.
  4. A handheld projector, with the right internal components, you could turn this into a way to make a portable projector, capable of projecting pictures on the go, perfect for imprompu presentations.
  5. A spray bottle cap, with adjustments allowing it to screw onto a watterbottle, a straw going to the bottom and a trigger it could be used for cleaning among other uses.
  6. A childrens toy, possibly in the form of an old fashinoned camera.
  7. Wall-mounted security camera.
  8. A hair drier, with extra room for components, it could be a high powered device.
  9. A gas despenser, with heliem or something similar it could be perfect for blowing up balloons for a party.
  10. If you placed a rotateable ball in the end of the cone, it could work as a massage gun as a convienient way to gently work out tightness in muscles.

Overall, I think the main ideal I took away from this project was patience. As tempting as it may be for me to rush through something like cutting out the shapes, it isn't conducive to a good end result. In fact, my lack of patience through those steps had caused me to mess up and I had to reprint and cut the templates again. If I were to attempt this project again, I would spend more time on the smaller aspects rather than getting blinded by the bigger picture. Slow and steady seems to be the best way to attain the results I want.
