Final Choice: Floating Pod

I chose this design because I liked its versatility and saw multiple uses for it. Furthermore, it had more purpose than the others as it could be used for private or public transport, used as an elevator or as a casual place to spend time with people. From there I began iterating the design

Original plans

After a basic idea of my desired function I began deciding on the form and aesthetic

Phase 2: Develop

Form sketch

I had devised an idea for the shape and form that I though best fit the idea.
I wanted the device to have an organic feel based on the shape of a seashell and to have an open-concept design. However I decided that it also needed to fit the enviorment so it needed safeguards against weather and saftey features

Moodboard

The key ideas for the design I decided on after my moodboard were:

Organic
Regal
Smooth
Round
Bright

I began considering materials that would fit with this vision, and decided on using wood that could be from repurposed driftwood, and glass to fit with the open-concept. since I had decided to base the form on a seashell I wanted to use nacre or mother of pearl for use as embelishment on the device as it could tie together the shape with the regal and bright themes.


I wanted to begin prototyping a physical model, but unfortunately my girlfriend had just gotten off a plane from the US the on 19th. I didn't want to risk spreading covid to anyone after being in close contact so I decided to play it safe and focus on making a digital prototype.

I began in Blender by making a flat plane making an aproximate shape and combining other shapes together to make a base. This took quite a while to find a good base form that resmbled a seashell. However, once I had it made I began filling in details.

Rather late in the process I realised I had missed placing the monitor inside, though it worked out as I adapted to have the monitor be retractable into the base as needed to conserve space. I found a picture online of a futuristic monitor and added an adress as if the pod was moving to it.

Monitor texture
Render with monitor

Now that I had solid renders showing all the different angles I wanted one final output to tie the functionality in with the project and really showcase it. I have never done animations in Blender before but I figured now was as good a time as any to learn. I created a small basic few story building and began assembling a timeline with the pod moving to it and escaliting to the third floor before pausing and descending. The trickiest part was getting the camera to follow along with the movement. However, after I finished I was thrilled with the result.

I had to use a different rendering engine, as Blender's cycle render would have taken over 8 whole days to render all 250 frames. As such, the quality of each frame is slightly less quality and some of the textures didn't function as they were supposed to.
